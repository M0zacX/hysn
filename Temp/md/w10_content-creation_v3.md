## What you are building

A complete HTML wireframe for the HYSN Content Creation & Animation page (`/services/content-creation`).

This is not a template-fill exercise. This is a strategic page design. Think first, then build.

---

## Think before you build

1. **Who lands on this page?** Two distinct visitors: (A) someone evaluating HYSN as a full package and wondering "do they also handle the content?", and (B) someone who already has screens (their own or competitor-installed) but is realizing the content is the actual problem. Both worry that good content will be expensive, slow, and require briefing meetings they don't have time for.

2. **What is the one thought they need to have to inquire?** "These people understand my business well enough to write good content for it. I don't have to brief them in detail. The result will look professional, not amateur. The price will fit a real business budget."

3. **What would actually make them inquire?** Visible content examples (screen mockups showing what HYSN actually produces). A clear briefing-light process — "we don't need a 50-page brand guide." Industry-specific examples (restaurant menu vs retail ad vs municipal info). The ability to imagine their own content on a screen.

4. **What would stop them?** No visible content examples. Vague "we create great content" claims. Worry that the process requires their team to do most of the work. Fear of "designed by committee" outcomes — the kind of corporate visual language nobody actually wants.

Now design the page that answers (3) and removes (4).

## Elevation permission

Sections worth considering:

- A **content example viewer** — interactive-feel placeholder showing different content rotating (menu board → retail ad → city info) — strongest possible proof element
- A **briefing simplicity story** — explicit section: "What we need from you" (just 3 things: your menu/products/services, your visual identity if you have one, your screen specs). Removes the "this will require a 5-page brief" fear.
- A **animation samples** — looping animation placeholders showing the level of motion design HYSN delivers
- A **production timeline** — typical content delivery time per package size
- A **template library reference** — for clients with smaller budgets, HYSN may offer pre-built templates that get adapted to their brand (faster, cheaper, still professional)

Add only if it elevates. Drop or replace if you have stronger alternatives — explain in HTML comment.

## Depth instructions

### Use cases section
4 content scenarios with operational specificity:

- **Restaurant Menüboard:** Food photography (HYSN organizes food shoot or works from existing photos), category structure, price hierarchy, animated daily specials, seasonal switches. What's the typical scope? Number of dishes? Update frequency?
- **Retail Werbescreen:** Product visuals, promotional graphics, animated offers, integration with paid advertising slots. How does the content rotate between own promotions and paid third-party advertising? What aspect ratios and resolution?
- **Stadt-Infostele:** City information layout, event announcements, local advertiser slots, possibly emergency information templates. What's the typical content split? How is content scheduled around city events?
- **Corporate Reception / Office:** Brand reel, welcome screens, internal news, KPI displays. Lower-volume but higher-polish requirements. How does HYSN balance brand consistency with content freshness?

Each card: scenario label + operational specificity (3–4 sentences).

### Testimonials section
Three voices specifically valuing the content side:

- A restaurant owner talking about food photography quality (e.g. "Wir hatten Handyfotos. HYSN hat zwei Stunden bei uns fotografiert und das Menüboard sieht jetzt aus wie aus einer Werbung — aber mit unseren echten Gerichten.")
- A retail or marketing voice about update speed (e.g. "Wenn wir eine Aktion launchen wollen, schicken wir die Daten an HYSN und am nächsten Tag läuft die Animation. Früher haben wir mit einer Agentur zwei Wochen daran gearbeitet.")
- A small business owner about cost-vs-quality (e.g. "Eine Agentur hatte uns für die Animation 3.500 € angeboten. HYSN hat es im Paket gemacht — gleiche Qualität, ein Bruchteil des Preises.")

German, client voice, specific.

### Vorher/Nachher case study
DIY phone photos / poorly formatted content vs HYSN-produced animated content for the same business. Visual comparison + sales/customer reaction impact.

### FAQ
Real content-related questions: do we need to provide our own photos, what file formats do you deliver, can we update the content ourselves later, do you do the food photography, what if we don't have a logo / brand guide, can we get just animation without content design, what's the revision cycle, what aspect ratios do you support, can you adapt content for different screen sizes.

- Section labels: `[SECTION NAME]` 10px uppercase gray
- Image placeholders: gray rectangle with diagonal SVG cross + German caption
- Buttons: 2px solid #333, no fill, padding 12px 28px
- Annotations: sticky-note boxes right margin, every section gets one
- Sections separated by 1px #d0d0d0 border
- Desktop only

## Think before you build

1. **Who lands on this page?** A franchise-system decision-maker — a Franchise-Geber (system owner), a Operations Director of a multi-location chain, a Marketing Director responsible for brand consistency across 5–50+ locations, or an IT/Systems lead evaluating central content management. They are solution-aware: they already know they need this. They're evaluating who can actually pull off a multi-location rollout without the chaos.

2. **What is the one thought they need to have to inquire?** "These people understand the difference between selling one screen and rolling out 30 screens. Central control works. Pilot location is possible. The operational model holds up at scale."

3. **What would actually make them inquire?** Real multi-location rollout proof. Visible central content management story. Pilot-first approach. Per-location pricing structure (even if not exact numbers). Clear handling of branch-level customization (regional specials, language, local promotions).

4. **What would stop them?** Anything that suggests HYSN is built for one-off installations. No multi-location proof. Worry that brand consistency breaks down across locations. Fear that branches will go rogue with the screens. Unclear cost scaling.

Now design the page that answers (3) and removes (4).

## Depth instructions

### Use cases section
Reason through these 4 franchise scenarios with multi-location specificity:

- **Quick-Service Gastronomie-Franchise (15+ locations):** Identical menu boards across all branches, central campaign deployment (e.g. summer promo launches simultaneously at all 15 sites on Monday morning), regional language variations (Berlin vs München specials), branch managers can swap in local items. Real operational mechanics.
- **Einzelhandel-Filialen (5–20 locations):** Window displays + interior signage. HQ pushes campaign creative, branch managers adjust to their store layout. How is in-store inventory tied to the screen content (e.g. featured product is always in stock)?
- **Apotheken-Verbund:** Strict regulatory content requirements (Heilmittelwerbegesetz), seasonal health campaigns, prescription pickup status. Central compliance + branch flexibility for local promotions.
- **Tankstellen-Netz / Convenience:** Outdoor pylon screens + indoor near kasse, dynamic fuel pricing display, food/coffee promotions, integration with loyalty system. High frequency of content updates.

Each card: franchise type label + scenario headline + 3–4 sentences with multi-location operational specificity (HQ mechanics, branch mechanics, scale).

### Testimonials section
Three voices from franchise systems. Right register sounds operationally specific:

- A Operations Director of a fast-food franchise talking about rollout speed (e.g. "Wir haben in 6 Monaten 23 Filialen ausgerüstet. Der Aufwand für mein Team war wesentlich geringer als beim letzten Anbieterwechsel — HYSN hat den Rollout-Plan strukturiert geliefert.")
- A Marketing Director of a retail chain talking about campaign control (e.g. "Wenn wir früher eine neue Aktion starten wollten, hat es zwei Wochen gedauert, bis alle Filialen dieselben Plakate hingen. Jetzt drücken wir am Montagmorgen einen Knopf und alle Standorte zeigen dasselbe.")
- A Franchise-Geber talking about brand consistency (e.g. "Manche Filialleiter sind kreativer als sie sein sollten. Mit dem zentralen System kann ich Kerninhalte sperren und nur lokale Aktionen freigeben — Markenführung ohne ständige Diskussion.")

German, multi-location operations register — specific, practical, no fluff.

### Vorher/Nachher case study
Construct a representative multi-location transformation. Vorher: each location did its own thing, brand inconsistency, slow campaign rollouts. Nachher: unified system, X locations, central content management, time-to-deploy reduced from weeks to minutes. Real or representative numbers.

### FAQ
Real franchise decision-maker questions: can we start with a pilot location, what happens if we only have 5 branches vs 50, who pays per-location (HQ or franchisee), can branch managers override central content, what languages are supported, what about international locations, how is the central CMS access managed, can we lock certain content from branch editing, what's the contract structure (per location vs master agreement), what happens when we open new locations.

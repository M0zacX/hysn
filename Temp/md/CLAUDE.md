## How to think about every page

Internalize these before you write a single word of copy or HTML.

**The customer is always the hero. HYSN is the guide.**
Never write as if HYSN is the hero of its own story. The restaurant owner, the supermarket manager, the municipality is the hero with a problem. HYSN is the experienced guide with a plan that helps them win. This must be felt in every headline, section intro and CTA.

**Grunt Test on every hero.**
A complete stranger must understand in 5 seconds what HYSN does, for whom, and why it matters. If a headline requires context to make sense, rewrite it.

**Match the awareness level of the audience.**
- Problem-aware (restaurants, offices, events): headline starts at the problem
- Solution-aware (franchise, retail): headline starts at the transformation
- Nearly unaware (municipalities, location partners): headline opens a possibility they did not know existed

**Pain moves away, not toward.**
Pain points are framed as what the customer loses or risks, not what they could gain. Loss aversion is the stronger driver. "Every price change means reprinting everything" lands harder than "Digital menus are faster to update."

**Every CTA is specific.**
No generic "Jetzt anfragen" as a default. Every CTA label reflects what the visitor just read and what happens when they click. "Menüboard für mein Restaurant planen" is a CTA. "Kontakt aufnehmen" is not.

**Kill objections before they form.**
Every CTA section has one objection-killer line directly below the button. One sentence that removes the most likely reason someone would hesitate. Never put objection handling only in the FAQ.

**Before/after is the only proof structure that works.**
Case studies and references always follow Vorher/Nachher structure. First the pain, then the transformation. Numbers wherever possible. Without the before, the after has no meaning.

**Use cases make solutions concrete.**
Generic feature descriptions don't convert. Specific scenarios do. A döner shop owner and a café owner have different mental pictures of "digital menu board." Write for each one specifically.

## Page logic (StoryBrand + Value Equation)

Every page follows this logic in order:
1. Hero — here is your problem / here is the possibility (Grunt Test)
2. Proof — we have done this before (trust bar, stats, references)
3. Empathy — we understand your situation (pain points)
4. Plan — here is how it works (steps, solution blocks)
5. Action — here is what to do next (specific CTA)
6. Stakes — here is what happens if you don't act (mild, never fear-based)

The Value Equation runs in the background of every page:
- Maximize: dream outcome (what the client's business looks like after) + perceived likelihood (real proof, real references)
- Minimize: time delay (screens up fast) + effort (HYSN handles everything)

## Language & Tone — non-negotiable

- All visible page copy is in German
- Anrede: always "Sie" — never "du"
- Tone: professional, direct, solution-oriented — like a trusted B2B partner, not a salesperson
- No filler phrases, no empty marketing language, no "wir sind leidenschaftlich"
- Every sentence must earn its place — if it doesn't inform or persuade, cut it
- Annotations and HTML comments can be in English (notes for the team, not visible to end users)

---

## Things you never do

- Never use scarcity tactics, countdown timers, or urgency manipulation — this is a premium B2B brand
- Never use ALL CAPS for emphasis
- Never write testimonials that sound generic ("Sehr empfehlenswert!") — dummy quotes must be specific and credible
- Never put lorem ipsum anywhere — write the best realistic directional version and mark it with `(Beispielwert)` if uncertain
- Never make HYSN the hero — no "Wir sind die Besten", no self-congratulation
- Never end a CTA section without an objection-killer line
- Never write a case study without a Vorher/Nachher structure

## Wireframe style rules — when generating new wireframes or matching the existing style

- Single self-contained HTML file. No external dependencies. No frameworks.
- Grayscale palette only: `#ffffff`, `#f4f4f4`, `#d0d0d0`, `#333333`, `#111111`. No brand colors.
- Body background `#f0f0f0`, page wrapper background `#ffffff`, max-width 900px centered, with right margin for annotations
- Font: `system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif`
- Section labels top-left of every section: `[SECTION NAME]` in 10px uppercase gray
- Image placeholders: gray rectangle with diagonal SVG cross + caption in German
- Buttons: 2px solid #333, no fill, padding 12px 28px, clear label
- Annotations: sticky-note style boxes in the right margin (`position: absolute; left: calc(100% + 22px); width: 210px`) explaining strategic reasoning per section
- Sections separated by 1px #d0d0d0 border
- Desktop wireframe only — mobile responsiveness not required

The existing files (especially `w04_municipalities_v3.html`) are the reference for current style. Match their structure exactly when generating new wireframes (e.g. the missing w06).

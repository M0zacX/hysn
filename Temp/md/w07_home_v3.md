# HYSN Wireframe Brief — Startseite (Home)
# Page 07 of 16 — v3 — MOST IMPORTANT PAGE

## What you are building

A complete HTML wireframe for the HYSN homepage (`/`).

This is the most important page on the entire website. It is the first impression. It must work for every audience simultaneously — restaurant owners, retail managers, event planners, municipal decision-makers, office managers, franchise operators — without sounding generic to any of them. Think first, then build.

---

## Think before you build

1. **Who lands on this page?** Mixed audience. Some came from a referral and just want to verify HYSN is legitimate. Some came from an ad targeting a specific industry. Some are exploring digital signage broadly. Some are revisiting after a sales conversation. The home page must serve all of them.

2. **What are the three different user journeys?**
   - **The skeptic** (40% of visitors): "Are these people real? Have they done this before?" — needs immediate proof
   - **The self-qualifier** (40%): "Is this for businesses like mine?" — needs to find their industry fast
   - **The ready-buyer** (20%): "Where do I click to talk to someone?" — needs a fast path to CTA

3. **What is the one thought every visitor needs to have?** "These people understand my situation. They've actually done this. The path forward is clear and low-risk."

4. **What would actually make them inquire?**
   - Hero that passes Grunt Test in 5 seconds
   - Trust signals before any sales pitch
   - A clear way to find their industry
   - Real proof (not stock photos and stock claims)
   - A skeptic-moment section ("why HYSN and not just buy a Samsung at MediaMarkt")
   - Multiple CTA opportunities along the page

5. **What would stop them?**
   - Vague headline that requires context
   - Generic stock-photo aesthetic
   - No way to find their industry
   - Self-promoting "we are the best" tone
   - One CTA at the bottom only
   - No skeptic-moment — the silent objection lingers

Now design the page that answers (4) and removes (5).

---

## Mandatory baseline — the home page is allowed to be longer than other pages

This page must accomplish more than industry pages because it serves multiple audiences at once. Build all of these:

1. **Nav bar** — sticky, primary CTA always reachable
2. **Hero** — the most important section on the entire website. Grunt Test critical. Must pass for a stranger in 3 seconds. Strongest possible image. Primary + secondary CTA. Objection killer.
3. **Trust bar** — 4 strong stats with full proof sentences. Real numbers from HYSN's operating context (active screens in REWE/EDEKA, restaurant clients, recurring revenue context, years operating).
4. **The problem (empathy first)** — StoryBrand: HYSN shows it understands the customer's challenge before pitching anything. 3–4 sentences from the customer's perspective. 3 bullet symptoms.
5. **HYSN as the guide (authority after empathy)** — 2–3 sentences + 3 authority proof points. Why HYSN is qualified. No self-congratulation — only specific proof.
6. **Industries we serve** — 6 industry cards in 2×3 grid: Restaurants | Handel | Events | Kommunen | Büro | Franchise. Each: industry name + 1 real benefit sentence + link. This is the self-qualification moment.
7. **The plan (how it works)** — 4 numbered steps from first contact to running screen. Reduces uncertainty.
8. **Use cases mini-grid** — 4–6 quick scenarios across industries showing HYSN screens in real contexts (see depth instruction)
9. **Vorher/Nachher reference teaser** — one strong real example with before/after, link to /referenzen
10. **Skeptic-moment section** — direct (not defensive) treatment of "why HYSN and not buy a Samsung at MediaMarkt or hire a local AV installer." 3 concrete differentiators.
11. **Testimonials** — 3 dummy quotes spanning different industries (see depth instruction)
12. **DOOH / hyads teaser** — for location partners and municipalities, screens can generate revenue. Short, with link to /partner-programm.
13. **What happens if nothing changes (stakes)** — 2–3 calm sentences. No pressure. Real consequence.
14. **Final CTA section** — strong closing. Primary CTA + secondary CTA (e.g. WhatsApp). Objection killer. 3 trust point pills.
15. **Footer** — full HYSN footer with 4 columns

## Elevation permission — this is the page where investments in extra sections pay off most

Sections to seriously consider for the home page:

- A **screen content rotation showcase** — animated/scrolling visual showing diverse HYSN content (menu board → retail ad → city info → event LED) — proves capability across industries in seconds
- A **a "wo unsere Bildschirme bereits laufen" map** — Germany map with markers showing real client locations (REWE, EDEKA, restaurants) — geographic proof
- A **before/after slider** — interactive vorher/nachher comparison — strongest possible proof element
- A **press / partner logos strip** — if any logos can be shown, this builds instant credibility
- A **"the HYSN difference" 3-column comparison** — HYSN vs hardware-only sellers vs DIY route — kills competitive comparison
- A **video testimonial placeholder section** — visual evidence that real clients exist
- A **partner / accreditation section** — Samsung Display Partner, LG Authorized, etc. — manufacturer credibility

Add anything that elevates. The home page benefits from more proof, not less. Do not bloat with marketing fluff — only add sections that move the needle.

If a section in the mandatory list feels redundant or weaker than your alternative, replace it — explain in HTML comment.

## Depth instructions

### The hero
This headline is the most-edited copy in the entire website. Spend real thought on it. It must:
- Pass Grunt Test in 3 seconds for a stranger
- Make sense to a restaurant owner AND a city decision-maker simultaneously
- Avoid generic phrases like "digitale Lösungen" or "moderne Kommunikation"
- Land at "what the customer gets," not "what HYSN sells"
- Be max 8 words

The subline expands the hero in 25 words max. Who, for whom, what they get.

### Use cases mini-grid
Don't make this a duplicate of industry pages. The home version is faster and broader — 4–6 quick scenarios that show range, each with one image placeholder + scenario label + 1–2 sentence description. Example range:
- Döner-Theke mit drei Menüboards
- REWE-Eingangsbereich mit Werbe-Stele
- Messestand mit LED-Wand
- Stadt-Fußgängerzone mit Outdoor-Stele
- Empfang einer Rechtsanwaltskanzlei
- Burger-Franchise mit zentral gesteuerten Filialscreens

Each one: scenario label + 1–2 sentences making it concrete.

### Skeptic-moment section
The silent question every visitor has is: "Why HYSN? Can't I just buy a screen at MediaMarkt and put it up?" Address this head-on. Three concrete differentiators that justify HYSN over alternatives:

1. The first differentiator addresses the hardware-only seller (e.g. screens are easy, *running content that converts* is the actual job)
2. The second addresses the DIY route (e.g. installation, mounting, content scheduling, hardware lifecycle — these become invisible costs when self-managed)
3. The third addresses generic AV installers (e.g. HYSN combines hardware + content + advertising monetization, not just installation)

Tone: confident, not defensive. Direct, not snide.

### Testimonials section
Three voices from different industries to show range. Each must have specificity that only a real client would mention:

- A **restaurant owner** with a specific operational truth (price update speed, customer reaction, sales impact)
- A **retail or municipal voice** with revenue specificity (monthly income, advertiser type, surprise upside)
- A **office or event voice** with installation/result specificity (time to install, visitor reaction, employer branding moment)

Each: realistic German quote + name + role + business + city. Real specificity, not marketing voice.

### Vorher/Nachher reference teaser
Pick the strongest single real or representative HYSN project. Vorher (one sentence). Nachher (one sentence with a number where possible). Link to full /referenzen page. Don't over-explain — this is a teaser pulling people deeper.

## Wireframe style rules — non-negotiable

- Single self-contained HTML file. No external dependencies.
- Grayscale palette: `#ffffff`, `#f4f4f4`, `#d0d0d0`, `#333333`, `#111111`
- Body `#f0f0f0`, page wrapper `#ffffff`, max-width 900px centered, 250px right margin
- Font: `system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif`
- Section labels: `[SECTION NAME]` 10px uppercase gray
- Image placeholders: gray rectangle with diagonal SVG cross + German caption
- Buttons: 2px solid #333, no fill, padding 12px 28px
- Annotations: sticky-note boxes right margin, every section gets one explaining strategy
- Sections separated by 1px #d0d0d0 border
- Desktop only

## Language
Visible page in German. Sie. Real copy. Hero headline must be agonized over — write it last after you've written everything else and have a feel for the page voice. Annotations English OK.

## Output
Single complete HTML file. The home page is allowed to be the longest single page in the site. Quality over compression — give every section the room it needs. Use cases, testimonials, skeptic-moment, and hero are the four sections that earn or lose this page.

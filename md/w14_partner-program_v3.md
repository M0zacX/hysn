A complete HTML wireframe for the HYSN Partner Program page (`/partner-programm`).

---

## Think before you build

1. **Who lands on this page?** A potential location partner — supermarket operator, building owner, gym/fitness operator, gas station owner, restaurant chain, municipality referent. They're nearly unaware: most don't know their footfall is monetizable through screens. They came from the home page DOOH teaser, a sales conversation, or a referral.

2. **What is the one thought they need to have to inquire?** "This is a real revenue stream. Zero effort on my side. HYSN handles everything. I can start with one screen to test, no big commitment."

3. **What would actually make them inquire?** Realistic revenue numbers for their location type. Clear "what HYSN handles vs what I handle" — and what they handle should be near zero. A pilot-first approach. Real partner case studies. Confidence about advertiser quality (no embarrassing ads).

4. **What would stop them?** Skepticism about realistic income. Worry about contract length. Fear that advertising looks cheap or undermines their brand. Concern about operational responsibility creeping in. Lack of social proof from similar location types.

Now design the page that answers (3) and removes (4).

## Mandatory baseline

This page overlaps with the DOOH page partially — but is more focused, deeper on the partner side, and includes details that don't belong on the dual-audience DOOH page.

1. **Nav bar** — sticky
2. **Hero** — opens the possibility for nearly-unaware audience: "Ihre Standortfrequenz wird zur Einnahmequelle". Visible image of digital screen at a supermarket or high-footfall location. Primary CTA: "Standort prüfen lassen". Objection killer about no investment risk and zero operational effort.
3. **What location partners lose every month** — pain-away framing: every customer walking past your store/lobby/gym without seeing an advertising screen is uncaptured advertising potential

10. **Mid-page CTA strip — pilot location**
11. **Real partner case study — Vorher/Nachher** — a real partner's transformation, with realistic monthly revenue context
12. **Testimonials** — 3 dummy quotes from partners (see depth instruction)
13. **Advertising standards reassurance** — explicit section: HYSN curates advertisers, no inappropriate content, partners can veto specific advertisers, content rotation balance with partner's own messaging
14. **FAQ** — 7 real partner questions
15. **Stakes + final CTA** — every month without a screen is a month of unrealized potential
16. **Footer**

## Depth instructions

### Who is a good fit — 6 location types
Reason through which locations actually generate good DOOH revenue:

- **Supermarkt-Eingangsbereich:** 800–2.500 daily customers, captive 30-second window while entering/leaving, high local advertiser interest
- **Fitnessstudio-Empfang:** 150–500 daily members, captive 5–15 min window during check-in/wait, advertiser fit (sports nutrition, clinics, sports retail)
- **Tankstelle / Convenience:** 500–1.500 daily customers, very short window but high local interest, advertiser fit (local restaurants, car services, lokale dienstleister)
- **Bürogebäude-Lobby:** 200–800 daily white-collar workers, captive 1–2 min while entering, premium advertiser fit (financial services, executive products, business lunch)
- **Apotheke / Gesundheitspraxis:** 100–400 daily visitors, longer wait windows, advertiser fit (pharma OTC, health products, local services)
- **Stadt-Standort (öffentlicher Raum):** thousands of daily passersby, public information + commercial advertising mix, municipal partnership structure

Each as a card with criteria + realistic context.

### Use cases — realistic monthly income examples
4 scenarios with realistic numbers (mark clearly as orientation, not guarantee):

- **Großer Supermarkt (REWE/EDEKA-Format):** ~1.500 daily customers, screen at entrance, 4–6 active advertisers, typical monthly revenue range (e.g. 280 € – 480 €). Operational effort: zero, HYSN handles everything.
- **Fitnessstudio mit ~250 täglichen Mitgliedern:** screen in reception area, 3–5 active advertisers, typical monthly revenue range (e.g. 120 € – 240 €). Member-targeted ad mix.
- **Tankstelle in mittlerer Lage:** ~800 daily customers, screen near kasse, 4–6 active advertisers, typical monthly revenue range (e.g. 180 € – 320 €).
- **Apotheke in Innenstadtlage:** ~250 daily customers, screen in waiting area, 3–4 active advertisers, typical monthly revenue range (e.g. 90 € – 180 €).

All numbers marked as orientation. Real numbers should reflect HYSN's actual operating context.

### Testimonials section
Three voices specifically about being a partner:

- A supermarket operator about the realistic income surprise (e.g. "Im ersten Quartal habe ich nicht damit gerechnet, dass die Werbeeinnahmen so stabil sein würden. Es ist nicht das Hauptgeschäft — aber es ist mehr, als ich für ungenutzte Wandfläche jemals erwartet hätte.")
- A fitness studio owner about brand fit (e.g. "Was uns wichtig war: keine billige Werbung. HYSN hat uns die Werbepartner vorgestellt, wir konnten Sport-relevante Marken priorisieren. Unsere Mitglieder finden den Bildschirm passend.")
- A small property owner about effort and operations (e.g. "Mein einziger Aufwand war ein 30-Minuten-Termin. Seit der Installation habe ich nichts gemacht — und am Monatsende kommt die Abrechnung.")

German, partner voice, specific.

### Vorher/Nachher case study
A real or representative partner transformation. Vorher: empty wall space, no advertising revenue, location aesthetically static. Nachher: digital screen running, X € monthly revenue, modernized aesthetic. Realistic numbers.

### FAQ
Real partner questions: how long is the contract, what happens if I want to terminate, who pays for electricity, can I block specific advertisers, can I show my own content, what if no advertisers want my location, how is the revenue paid out, what's the typical timeline from inquiry to first revenue, what if my location frequency is unclear, can I expand to multiple locations.

## Wireframe style rules — non-negotiable

- Single self-contained HTML file. No external dependencies.
- Grayscale palette: `#ffffff`, `#f4f4f4`, `#d0d0d0`, `#333333`, `#111111`
- Body `#f0f0f0`, page wrapper `#ffffff`, max-width 900px centered, 250px right margin
- Font: `system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif`
- Section labels: `[SECTION NAME]` 10px uppercase gray
- Image placeholders: gray rectangle with diagonal SVG cross + German caption
- Revenue example calculations: highlighted in a subtle box with "Orientierungswert — keine Garantie" label
- Buttons: 2px solid #333, no fill, padding 12px 28px
- Annotations: sticky-note boxes right margin, every section gets one
- Sections separated by 1px #d0d0d0 border
- Desktop only

## Language
Visible page in German. Sie. Real copy. Tone: business-to-business, factual, no overpromise. Annotations English OK.

## Output
Single complete HTML file. The partner program page must convince location operators that this is real, low-risk income — without sounding like a get-rich-quick pitch.
